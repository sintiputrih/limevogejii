Bill Bellamy- Biography

Bill Bellamy is a well-known American stand-up comedian, actor, and television host. He is well-known for his appearances on shows such as Def Comedy Jam.

William Bellamy, better known as Bill Bellamy, is a stand-up comedian, actor, video jockey, and television host from the United States of America. He pioneered the booty call phase and appeared on HBO’s Russell Simmons’ Def Comedy Jam. Bill Bellamy starred in films such as Joey Breaker, Who’s the Man, Fled, How to Be a Player, The Brothers, The Bounce Back, A Dark Foe, and more. He also appeared in notable television shows such as Cousin Skeeter, Men, Women, and Dogs, Fastlane, Castle, Royal Pains, Insecure, SWAT, and others.

Bill Bellamy- Professional Career

Bill is a well-known American comedian. Since the 1980s, he has grown in popularity among his audience. He began his career as a regular on MTV and later became a VJ there. Bill Bellamy hosted major MTV shows like MTV Jamz and MTV Beach House. He made his acting debut in the 1993 film Joey Breaker, playing the character Ace Man. Bill Bellamy appeared in the film Who’s the Man? During the same calendar year. In the 1996 film Fled, he portrayed Ray.

He then appeared in How to Be a Player in 1997. In 1999, he appeared in two films: Love Stinks and Any Given Sunday. Bill is also a well-known television personality. From 1998 to 2001, he appeared on the Nickelodeon TV sitcom Cousin Skeeter. He appeared in two Kenan and Kel episodes. Men, Women, and Dogs, a TV sitcom in which he starred from 2001 to 2002, earned him his big break.

In the Fox Network TV show Fastlane, he appeared alongside Tiffani Thiessen and Peter Facinelli. He has also appeared in series such as Buying the Cow and Chelsea Lady.

In 2007 and 2008, Bill hosted the fifth and sixth seasons of the hit NBC reality show Last Comic Standing. He had an appearance in Hot in Cleveland in 2014. In the 2016 film Kindergarten Cop 2, he played FBI agent Sanders. He was the host of the immensely popular comic show Bill Bellany’s Who’s Got Joke? Following that, he appeared in films such as Mr. Box Office, The Bounce Back, Winning Ugly, Insecure, A Dark Foe, and more. He also appears in the Netflix series Self Made in 2020.

Bellamy has been performing standup since he was a freshman at Rutgers University, first in New York at The Improv, then in Los Angeles at The Comic Strip and The Comedy Store.
