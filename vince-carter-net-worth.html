Vince Carter | Career

Carter had a 22 season long career in NBA, which is a rare feat to achieve by an athlete at such a top level.

Initially, Carter started his career in 1998 when he got drafted by Golden State Warriors with a fifth overall pick in NBA Draft.

Vince played seven seasons for the Toronto Raptors from 1998 to 2004 and managed to feature five times in NBA All-Star.

Likewise, he managed to play for several other NBA clubs in his career and recorded unforgettable performances helping his team win.

While playing for Atlants Hawks in the 2019-20 season, he became the first player in NBA to play in four different decades.

Meanwhile, the 2000-01 season was best for him as Vince managed a remarkable 27.6 points per game that helped his team Raptors reach the semifinals.

Sadly, the former NBA great finally decided to hang his boot in 2020.

Besides his career in NBA, Carter managed to showcase his talent for the United States of America and helped his team with two Olympic gold medals.
