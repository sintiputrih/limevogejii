Are Aaron Judge Rookie Cards are Undervalued?

Aaron Judge (April 26, 1992) was born in Linden, California, and is an outfielder New York Yankees. Even though he is one of the biggest names in the sport, his rookie cards and autographs have long been deemed undervalued in the collectibles market. Below we look at Aaron Judge's card value, his career so far, and what we know about collecting cards of the MLB superstar.


The Baseball Superstar Aaron Judge

The impact of Aaron Judge on baseball began with a burst like a rocket in 2017. Hammering out 52 home runs, 128 runs scored, and 114 RBIs, he put up an unbelievable campaign that highlighted him as potentially the best talent in the game, earning him Rookie of the Year honors and establishing him as an MVP-caliber player. He is loved as one of the best players in the game due to his consistency and throwing capability, with strong career averages and multiple All-Star selections.


The New York Yankee Legacy

Aaron Judge's code-players for one of the most iconic franchises in the sport blessed him with a larger celebrity and fan base for sure. His commitment to the organization has added another layer to his legend-making him synonymous with the franchise, and more of a draw for collectible cards.


Aaron Judge Card Values

Despite his sensational career and year-in and year-out excellence, Aaron Judge's rookie cards and autographs are often overlooked in the current collectibles market. In particular his 2017 Topps Chrome Rookie Autograph and Topps Chrome Rookie PSA Gem Mint 10.


Finding Undervalued Cards

Collectors and investors might be able to find underpriced Aaron Judge cards if they look in places like eBay and auction houses. Also look for variations that seem undervalued and evaluate trends of color parallels and numbered cards, as well as autos. 
To the very end, Aaron Judge should serve as an important figure in the baseball world as one of the best young power hitters to burst onto the scene and play with the storied New York Yankees. Even with that recognition his rookie cards and autographs are frequently described as underrated, making for a tasty prospect for collectors and investors alike.
