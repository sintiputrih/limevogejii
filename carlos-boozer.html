Who is Carlos Boozer: Biography, Net Worth &amp; more

Carlos Boozer is a $60 million net-worth American professional basketball player. Carlos Boozer played for the Cleveland Cavaliers, Utah Jazz, Chicago Bulls, and Los Angeles Lakers throughout his NBA career.

He also competed for the United States men’s national basketball team, where he won a bronze medal in the 2004 Summer Olympics and a gold medal in the 2008 Summer Olympics. From 2016 to 2017, Boozer was a member of the Guangdong Southern Tigers in China.

Who is Carlos Boozer?

Carlos Boozer was born on November 20, 1981, as one of five children to Carlos Sr. and Renee on a military base in Aschaffenburg, West Germany. He grew up in Juneau, Alaska. Boozer attended Juneau-Douglas High School as a kid, where he led the Crimson Bears basketball team to two straight state championships.

Boozer was traded to the Chicago Bulls in the summer of 2010. Despite missing 23 games due to injury in his first season with the team, he still averaged 17.5 points and 9.6 rebounds per game. Furthermore, Boozer contributed to the Bulls’ first-place finish in the Eastern Conference. In the 2011-12 season, his averages dipped to 15 points and 8.5 rebounds per game. In 2012-13, Boozer improved with the Bulls, averaging 16.2 points and 9.8 rebounds in 79 games. The Bulls waived him in 2014.

Following his release from the Bulls, Boozer was grabbed off of amnesty waivers by the Los Angeles Lakers. In 2014-15, he played for the team for one season. Boozer finished his career in China with the Guangdong Southern Tigers of the Chinese Basketball Association. He announced his retirement in December of 2017.
