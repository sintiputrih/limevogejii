Aidan Hutchinson Bio: NFL, Girlfriend &amp; More!

Aidan Hutchinson is a defensive end for Detroit Lions in NFL, making waves with his impressive defensive skills.

Hutchinson got his NFL inspiration from his father, a former college football player at Michigan University.

His college football career at Michigan was nothing short of legendary. As a senior in 2021, he took home several prestigious awards, including the Ted Hendricks and Lombardi Awards.

Early Life

On August 9, 2000, Aidan Hutchinson was born in Plymouth, Michigan, to Chris Hutchinson (Father) and Melissa Sinkevics (Mother).

His father, Chris, played defensive lineman for the University of Michigan. Similarly, his mother, Melissa, won the 1988 Miss Michigan Teen USA event.

He also has two older sisters:  Aria and Mia Hitchinson. His sister, Aria, won Miss Michigan USA 2022.

For education, Aidan attended Divine Child High School in Dearborn, Michigan, and committed to playing college football at his father’s alma mater.

Though well-known for his athletic capabilities, Aidan was also academically good. He was a three-time Academic All-Big Ten honoree and holds a bachelor’s degree in applied exercise science from the University of Michigan.

Like Father, Like Son

Aidan Hutchinson looks up to his father, Chris Hutchinson, as his inspiration and role model.

Chris was a successful player, serving as a tri-captain during his senior year at Michigan in 1992 and earning the team’s Most Valuable Player award.

Furthermore, he was named the Big Ten’s Defensive Lineman of the Year and an All-American.

Aidan’s interest in football started early on.

In seventh grade, he had a goal sheet taped to his wall just below his father’s #97 Rose Bowl game jersey, which said, “I will play football at the University of Michigan.” In 2018, his dreams did come true.

Aidan has inherited his father’s skills and is determined to achieve his goals, even wearing his father’s jersey number, 97.

Despite playing differently due to his unique frame, Chris recognizes similarities between his son’s playing style and his own.

Early Career

Aidan’s career started at high school, where he played multiple positions, including defensive end, tight end, offensive line, and long snapper. He also played in the 2018 U.S. Army All-American Game.

Hutchinson committed to playing college football at the University of Michigan and joined the team in 2018 as a true freshman.

He was a great player from his starting years, but unfortunately, he suffered a season-ending injury that sidelined him for an entire year.

However, he bounced back in his senior year, setting the team’s single-season sack record at 14, a remarkable feat that earned him several awards and recognition.

Professional Career

Following his standout senior year, Hutchinson declared for the 2022 NFL Draft, where the Detroit Lions drafted him with the second overall pick.

Despite being a rookie, he set a new Lions rookie record by recording three sacks in his second game.

His impressive rookie season earned him a place on the All-Rookie team of Pro Football Writers of America (PFWA).

Furthermore, he won the 2022 Pepsi NFL Rookie of the Year, making him one of the league’s most promising young players.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQS)

Did Aidan Hutchinson win Rookie of the Year?

Yes, Aidan Hutchinson won Pepsi Zero Sugar Rookie of the Year. But, to be clear, this award is fan-voted, not the same thing as the Defensive Rookie of the Year award, which is given out at the NFL Honors event and is voted on by the Associated Press. 

What are the NFL career statistics of Aidan Hutchinson?

In the 2022 NFL season, Aidan Hutchinson had 52 total tackles, 9.5 sacks, 3 pass deflections, and 3 interceptions in his 17 games with the Lions.
