Grace Anna Sings - Age, Family, Bio

Singer who became an internet sensation with her renditions of songs like "The Star-Spangled Banner," "Zip-a-Dee-Doo-Dah" and Charlie Puth's "One Call Away," which she posts on her Grace Anna Sings YouTube channel. She suffers from a rare form of dwarfism called Conradi Hunermann syndrome.

Before Fame

She was raised in Dunnville, Kentucky. She was born blind due to cataracts

Trivia

 She sang "The Star-Spangled Banner" at the Military Veteran Project's Salute To Our Heroes Gala in Topeka, Kansas in November 2014.

Family Life

Her parents' names are Jeff and Angie. She has a brother named Isaiah. 

Associated With

Her version of Lee Greenwood's "Proud to Be an American" is one of her most popular covers.
