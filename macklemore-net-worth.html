Macklemore: Career

Beginnings of musical Career

Benjamin Hammond Haggerty, aka Macklemore, began his music career when he was 15 and used to write his own lyrics.

Further, he often sang with his college group named Elevated Elements in the events organized in The Evergreen State College.

In addition, Haggerty dropped an album entitled Progress in 2000 with his group, which sparked his aspiration of becoming a full-time musician.

As a matter of fact, he dropped his first-ever recorded mixtape, Open Your Eyes, in October 2000. At that time, he used the name Professor Macklemore.

Following this mixtape launch, Haggerty changed his stage name from Professor Macklemore to just Macklemore.

After a while, Haggerty, aka Macklemore, started to focus on recording his solo studio album.

Subsequently, American hip hop artist Macklemore dropped his first-ever studio album entitled The Language of My World.

Nonetheless, this debut album by Macklemore captivated the eye of many record producers, including Ryan Lewis, with whom he has released a couple of albums as of now.

As a result, Macklemore and Lewis instantly clicked so well that they began to work together to release a studio album together.

To sum it up, Haggerty, aka Macklemore, dropped his second mixtape called The Unplanned Mixtape in 2009 that gained Macklemore a firm ground in the Music industry.

Ascend to Fame

Following his second mixtape release, American singer Macklemore and his fellow artist friend Ryan Lewis dropped a collaborative EP, The VS. EP, in late 2009.

Not to mention, they named the duo group Macklemore & Ryan Lewis and went on to perform and launch numerous tracks afterward.

Soon after, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis released a couple of songs entitled Stay At Home Da in March 2010 and Otherside in October 2010.

Subsequently, the duo released a track titled Can’t Hold Us, which became an iconic track in the hip hop music genre.

In fact, with the release of this song “Can’t Hold Us” on August 16, 2011, Benjamin Hammond Haggerty, aka Macklemore, rose to stardom.

On top of that, it sold over two million copies within few weeks following its release and has made about five million copies sales as of now.

As a matter of fact, Can’t Hold U topped numerous song charts worldwide, including US Billboard 200.

Macklemore & Ryan Lewis

Following his success with the song Can’t Hold U, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis released their debut studio album entitled The Heist.

Furthermore, The Heist was released on 9 October 2012 and peaked the second spot on thUS Billboard 200.

Above all, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis won outstanding four Grammy Awards in 2014 for these albums.

Not to add that, The Heist won the category of Best New Artist and Best Rap Album.

Additionally, the track Thrift Shop in this album earned the Grammy Award for the Best Rap Performance in 2014.

Afterward, the duo went on a world tour covering The Heist album, and they announced they were working for their second album together.

As a result, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis released their second album entitled This Unruly Mess I’ve Made in 2014.

Similar to their first album, it topped several music charts such as UK R&B Albums, Us Billboard Independent Album, and US Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums.

Sadly, the duo Macklemore and Ryan Lewis announced their splitting on June 15, 2017.
