What is Luka Modrić’s Net Worth and Salary?

Luka Modrić is a Croatian soccer player with a net worth of $75 million. He is widely considered one of the best midfielders of all time and the greatest Croatian player ever. He started his career with Dinamo Zagreb and later played for Tottenham in the English Premier League before joining Real Madrid in 2012.

Modrić quickly became an integral part of Real Madrid, helping the team win many Champions Leagues and other titles. He has won multiple awards for “Best Midfielder” and the Ballon d’Or. Modrić also captains the Croatian national team and led them to the 2018 World Cup final, where they ultimately lost against France.

Luka Modrić’s Notable Contracts

Luka Modrić has signed several notable contracts throughout his career. In 2008, he signed a six-year deal with Tottenham Hotspur. He later signed a five-year deal with Real Madrid in 2016. In 2020, he signed a one-year extension with Real Madrid, taking his contract through to the 2021-22 season.

While Modrić’s annual salary is not publicly disclosed, it is estimated to be around €10 million per year.

Luka Modrić’s Childhood and Early Years

Luka Modrić was born on September 9th, 1985 in Zadar, SR Croatia, SFR Yugoslavia. His childhood was disrupted by the Croatian War of Independence, during which his family was forced to flee their home. Luka’s grandfather was tragically shot by Serb rebels, and their family home was burned to the ground. The family lived as refugees for several years, with Luka turning to football as a means of escape.

Despite the chaos around him, Luka’s potential as a footballer became clear. His family scraped together what little money they had to fund his soccer training at a sports academy. Luka’s dedication and talent eventually led him to great success in the sport.

Modrić’s Career Journey

Luka Modrić began his football career with NK Zadar and later moved to Dinamo Zagreb as a 16-year-old. He won the Bosnian Premier League Player of the Year Award at the age of 18 before breaking through with Dinamo Zagreb’s first team. In 2008, he agreed to move to Tottenham Hotspur for a transfer fee of 16.5 million pounds. Initially, he struggled to establish himself in England but eventually proved his critics wrong under the management of Harry Redknapp.

In 2012, Real Madrid paid 30 million pounds to sign Modrić, who became one of the most influential players to ever wear the famous white jersey. Though he faced initial criticism while playing under manager José Mourinho, he won a place in the starting eleven by 2013. By 2017, he had won the Ballon d’Or and the Champions League for the fourth time with Real Madrid.

Luka Modrić’s Family Life

Luka Modrić is a private person and does not share much about his family life with the media. In 2010, he married Vanja Bosnić and they have three children together. The family follows the Roman Catholic faith.

Perjury Charge Against Modrić Dismissed

In a controversial trial for tax evasion involving former Dinamo Zagreb executive Zdravko Mamić, Luka Modrić took the stand as a witness. However, he allegedly gave incorrect dates for certain events related to his contracts with his former club, leading to a perjury charge against him. The public opinion on his innocence was split among Croats, with some supporting him and others seeing him as part of a wider corruption scandal. In 2018, the perjury charge against Modrić was dismissed.
