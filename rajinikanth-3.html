Some Lesser Known Facts About Rajinikanth

  • Does Rajinikanth smoke?: No (Quit)
  • Does Rajinikanth drink alcohol?: Yes
  • Rajnikanth is a Maharashtrian by birth and not a Tamilian, though his ancestors hailed from both Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu.
  • He lost his mother at a young age after which he was raised by his father and his elder brothers.

    Rajinikanth childhood photo

  • He was a very naughty kid in his childhood.
  • Before becoming an actor he did odd jobs in Chennai and Bengaluru such as a carpenter, a coolie, and a bus conductor for the Bangalore Transport Service (BTS). As a bus conductor, he used to get ₹750/month.
  • His friend, Raj Bahadur, used to give him funds to learn acting in a film institution in Chennai.

    Rajinikanth in younger days

  • As he was keen on acting, he joined the Madras Film Institute, and during one of his stage performances, he met the director, K Balachander, who offered him a role in his Tamil film. Until that time, he was not good at speaking Tamil, but he quickly learned it and master the language.

    Rajinikanth with K Balachander

  • He made his acting debut alongside Kamal Haasan in Tamil film ‘Apoorva Raagangal‘ (1975).

    Rajinikanth and Kamal Haasan in Apoorva Raagangal

  • For the initial two years of his acting career, he got recognised for his negative roles, until he got the lead role in the Telugu film ‘Chilakamma Cheppindi‘ (1977).
  • His first commercial success was ‘Billa’ (1980), a remake of Amitabh Bachchan’s ‘Don’ (1978).

    Rajinikanth’s Billa is a remake of Don

  • He met his wife Latha, when a group of college girls came to take his interview, where Latha was leading the group. Rajinikanth got so much attracted to Latha that he proposed her on the same day.
  • In his 100th film, he played the role of a Hindu saint ‘Raghavendra Swami’ in the film ‘Sri Raghavendra‘ (1985).

    Rajinikanth played the role of Raghavendra Swami

  • In 1988, Rajinikanth did his first and only English film ‘Bloodstone,’ an Indian-American action-adventure film.

    Rajinikanth acted in Bloodstone

  • His film ‘Raaja Chinna Roja‘ (1989), was the first Tamil film to use animation.

    Rajinikanth starred in Raaja Chinna Roja

  • His only film that was released with a U/A certificate was ‘Thalapathi‘ (1991).
  • In 2002, he did a day-long fast to protest the Karnataka Government’s decision not to release water from the river Kaveri into Tamil Nadu. Also in 2008, he participated in a day-long fast with other Tamil film personalities, demanding that the Sri Lankan government must end the civil war and give Sri Lankan Tamils their rights.

    Rajinikanth fast in 2002

  • In 2007, he became the highest paid Asian actor after Jackie Chan, when he was paid ₹26 crore for the film ‘Sivaji.’
  • His science fiction film ‘Enthiran‘ (English – Robot) was supposed to be done by Kamal Haasan.
  • Rajinikanth was named as the Most Influential Indian of 2010, by Forbes India.
  • He made a special appearance in Shah Rukh Khan‘s Sci-Fi film, ‘Ra.One‘ (2011).
  • In 2011, he supported social activist Anna Hazare’s anti-corruption movement and offered him to use of his marriage hall, Raghavendra Kalyana Mandapam in Chennai.
  • Since 1995, he goes to the Himalayas after every film.

    Rajinikanth in the Himalayas

  • He doesn’t meet people after 9 pm.
  • Rajinikanth is very punctual and reaches all his shootings before time.

    Rajinikanth is worshipped by his fans

    Rajinikanth with Meena

  • His much famous style of tossing cigarette came from an incidence when he wanted to join a gang in his school days, as they were his seniors so they refused, after which he thought he would impress the guys with this trick. He used to practice this trick in the bushes of the school.
    • He is the first Indian actor to have featured in black & white, coloured, animated, and 3D film.

  • On 25 October 2021, he was honoured with the prestigious Dadasaheb Phalke Award by the Government of India. The award was presented to him by the  Vice President of India Venkaiah Naidu. The actor dedicated his award to his mentor K Balachander, his brother Sathyanarayana Rao Gaikwad and his bus driver-friend Raj Bahadur. After receiving the award, in a short speech, he said,I dedicate this award to my guru and mentor K Balachander sir, and I remember him with gratitude. I also dedicate this to my my brother Thiru Sathyanarayana Rao Gaikwad, who was a father figure to me, and raised me with great values instilling spirituality in me. Raj Bahadur was the first person to identify the acting talent in me when I was a bus conductor, and encouraged me to enter the world of cinema. I’d also like to thank all my producers, directors, technicians, co-artistes, distributors, media and the press. They are the Gods who keep me living.”

    Rajinikanth receiving the prestigious Dadasaheb Phalke Award
