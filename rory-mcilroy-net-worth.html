What is Rory McIlroy’s Net Worth and Career Earnings?

Rory McIlroy, a professional golfer from Northern Ireland, has a net worth of $170 million. He has been ranked as the number-one professional golfer in the world for over 100 weeks and is a winner of several championships. By the age of 25, he was part of an exclusive group of golfers who have won three majors.

Most of Rory’s income comes from endorsements rather than tournament winnings, with an estimated annual income of $40-50 million. In 2019, he won the Tour Championship and FedEx Cup, earning $15 million.

Rory McIlroy’s Early Years

Rory McIlroy was born on May 4th, 1989, in Holywood, Northern Ireland. He started golfing at an early age and became the youngest ever member of his local golf club at age seven. His father, a skilled golfer, taught him how to play and even instructed him to hold his new golf club properly while he slept.

McIlroy’s family worked multiple jobs to provide him with the funding he needed to become a professional golfer. They sent him to his first major tournament, the World Championship for boys aged 9 and 10 at the Doral Golf Resort in Miami, where he emerged as the winner.

Rory McIlroy’s Early Golf Success

Rory McIlroy achieved tremendous success in golf at a young age. By age 15, he had won the Junior Ryder Cup in 2004 with the European team. He also won championships across Ireland, including the West of Ireland Championship and the Irish Close Championship, becoming the youngest winner in 2005. The following year, he won the European Amateur. McIlroy continued to compete in various tournaments, such as the Walker Cup, the British Masters, the Dubai Desert Classic, and the Open Championship.

Rory McIlroy’s Career Highlights

In 2007, McIlroy became a professional golfer. He joined the top 200 golfers in the world by the start of 2008. In 2009, he won his first tournament, the Dubai Desert Classic, and finished the year with a top ten world ranking.

In 2010, McIlroy became the first golfer since Tiger Woods to secure a PGA Tour win at the age of 21. He finished the year with a world ranking of seventh.

In 2011, McIlroy famously threw away victory in the final round of the Masters Tournament but bounced back with a stunning victory at the US Open. He held a world ranking of fourth after this win.

In 2012, McIlroy became the number one ranked golfer for the first time in his career. He would claim and lose the number one spot numerous times, eventually cementing his position as the best-ranked golfer.

In 2013 and the beginning of 2014, McIlroy’s performance was somewhat disappointing. However, he bounced back with wins at the Open Championship and the PGA Championship in 2014. He joined a group of only three professional golfers who had won ten PGA tour events and four majors by the age of 25.

After a winless streak in 2017, McIlroy won the Players Championship in 2019 and regained the number-one spot on the world rankings in 2020.

Rory McIlroy’s Impressive Earnings

Rory McIlroy has earned over $34 million in his golf career so far. He became the youngest player to earn €10 million on the European Tour by the age of 22 and broke the record for the youngest player to earn $10 million on the PGA Tour in 2012. SportsPro named him the “third most marketable athlete in the world” in 2013.

McIlroy reportedly signed a deal with Nike worth between $100 million and $250 million in 2013. In 2017, he signed a 10-year extension with Nike worth $200 million, followed by a $100 million deal with TaylorMade as an equipment endorsement when Nike stopped producing golf equipment. He won the FedEx Cup in 2016 and 2019, earning a record-breaking payout of $15 million in the latter year.

Between June 2017 and June 2018, McIlroy earned $40 million from salary and endorsements, with another $40 million earned through other income the following year. He ranked sixth on the list of highest-paid athletes in the world in 2019, with the majority of his income coming from endorsements. In the following year, he earned $50 million.
