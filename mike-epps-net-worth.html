On June 1, 2014, Mike was accused of hitting fellow stand-up comedian LaVar Walker outside the Uptown Comedy Club in Atlanta, Georgia.

Later, he expressed regret, claiming that it was entirely spontaneous and that he would never harm an animal.

Mike Epps | Career

When Mike moved to Brooklyn and joined the “Def Comedy Jam” tour in 1995, he got his big break.

He then appeared in two episodes of “Def Comedy Jam” on HBO. These appearances boosted his comic career and helped him become more well-known.

His first acting role was in “Strays” (1997), Vin Diesel‘s first movie as a director.

He was then chosen to star in the sequel to the “Friday” film series, “Next Friday” (1999).

Epps was given a chance to try out for the part of Day-Day Jones after Ice Cube saw one of his stand-up performances and extended the invitation.

Later, in the 2002 movie “Friday After Next,” he returned to the character of Day-Day Jones.

He also collaborated with Ice Cube in the movie “All About the Benjamins” the same year.

In the late 1990s and early 2000s, Epps worked in many movies like “How High” (2001), which featured Method Man and Redman, and “3 Strikes” (2000).
